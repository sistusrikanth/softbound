**Title:** The Gathering of the Golden Leaf

**World Context:** A quiet, sun-drenched meadow at twilight, where the air holds the scent of damp earth and the light glows like soft, warm honey.

**Characters:**
*   Pip (The Observer)
*   Elder Moss (The Keeper)
*   Elara (The Folder)

***

**Page 1**
*Visual:* A wide, watercolor view of the meadow. The sky is a soft, dusty indigo, and the grass is a deep, mossy green. Pip sits near a large, smooth stone.
*Text:* The meadow is very still. The day is folding itself away, tucking the light into the edges of the trees.

**Page 2**
*Visual:* Pip looks up. A single, golden leaf drifts slowly downward, turning in the air like a sleepy dancer.
*Text:* Pip watches a golden leaf drift down. It moves as slowly as a thought.

**Page 3**
*Visual:* The leaf lands softly on the grass near Elder Moss, who is resting with his eyes closed. His shell is covered in tiny, thriving clover.
*Text:* It lands near Elder Moss. He does not wake, but he hums a low, rumbling note that feels like a warm blanket.

**Page 4**
*Visual:* Elara lands gracefully beside the leaf. Her wings are thin and textured like parchment. She tilts her head, looking at the leaf with curiosity.
*Text:* Elara arrives on quiet wings. She looks at the leaf, seeing the patterns in its veins.

**Page 5**
*Visual:* Elara uses her beak to gently fold the tip of the leaf inward. It makes no sound.
*Text:* With a soft touch, she begins to fold. She turns the edge of the leaf, making it small and neat.

**Page 6**
*Visual:* Pip moves closer, sitting beside Elara. He watches the folding process with wide, calm eyes.
*Text:* Pip watches, breathing in time with the movement. One fold, then another.

**Page 7**
*Visual:* The leaf is now a delicate, folded shape. Elara places it carefully into a small hollow on the side of Elder Moss’s shell.
*Text:* The leaf is finished. It is a small, golden treasure, tucked safely into the garden on Elder Moss’s back.

**Page 8**
*Visual:* Elder Moss opens one eye slowly. He gives a gentle nod, his shell glowing faintly in the twilight.
*Text:* Elder Moss feels the weight of the gift. He hums again, a sound of thanks that ripples through the grass.

**Page 9**
*Visual:* The three friends sit together. The sky has deepened to a soft, velvet indigo, and the first star appears.
*Text:* There is nothing more to do. The meadow is safe, and the night is kind.

**Page 10**
*Visual:* A close-up of the meadow grass, slightly blurred. The light is dim and peaceful.
*Text:* Everything is exactly where it should be.

***

**End State:** The story settles into a state of profound stillness. The characters remain in the meadow, resting in the quiet dark. The page can be turned back to the beginning to watch the leaf drift down once more, or the reader may simply sit with the final image until they feel ready to move on.
